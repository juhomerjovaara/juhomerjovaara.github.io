Juho Merjovaara

26 years old. About 7 professional years in IT. 2 professional years in game development. Numerous gaming projects, both academical and personal. Game Development student from Finland.

My journey in IT started during my studies at Helsinki Business College. After completing my dual qualification in Vocational Qualification in Business Information Technology and completing the Finnish Matriculation Examination I was fortunate enough to get an internship position at Raha-automaattiyhdistys (RAY, Finland’s Slot Machine Association, these days Veikkaus Oy). During my internship I worked as a Testing Trainee on a program used to channel funds. At the same time I was in charge of the program’s customer service with another worker. After completing my internship, I was able to stay at RAY and my title was upgraded to System Tester. A few months later I was transferred to the unit I really wanted to work in all along, the game development branch. The testing team to be precise.

My responsibilities were mostly testing slot machine games, online casino games and payment methods. During my time I learned a lot of the gaming industry and realized that this was the field I want to work in. After RAY I stated working In a smaller point company called Crafthouse Oy. My title was for the first few years was System Specialist and I did things such as POS System implementation, customer training, general customer service via phone, email and remote-control software, customer version updates, on call duty etc. I knew I wanted to get back to the gaming field and applied to Metropolia University of Applied Sciences for their Game Development major a degree in Bachelor of Engineering.

I started school and continued working at Crafthouse Oy as a Software Tester. As the only tester I was in charge of the testing of multiple key products including POS systems, management software, web stores and POS hardware. During my studies I have learned a lot about game development, game design, programming (mostly C# and C++), game engines (mostly Unity), developing for different platforms (PC, mobile, VR, AR, consoles), 3D (Blender, Autodesk products), databases and a lot more. I have been a part of multiple school game projects and created games on my own.

At the end of 2018 I moved to Calgary, Canada for student exchange and studied at the Mount Royal University for the winter semester.

In 2019 I was a part of a group that is created an online game that combines VR and AR for Helsinki XR Center as a school project. My responsibilities were mostly in VR programming and level generation.

In 2020 I moved to Amsterdam to do an internship at DTT. I was a part of the Unity team and worked on the Edu Play Book project. My internship was unfortunately cut short due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

I have a lot of experience in the IT field and in non-professional gaming projects. I know I am ready to take the next step in my game development career.
